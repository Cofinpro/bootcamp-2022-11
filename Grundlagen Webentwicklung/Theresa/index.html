Some Facts

  • Body length: 37 - 62.5 cm
  • Tail length: 34 - 76.2 cm
  • Weight: 1.2 - 3 kg

African palm civets are nocturnal and sleep for hours high up in the trees after eating. Their diet includes mostly fruits but also smaller animals like insects and birds, dead or alive.

In the year 1830 they were first described by british zoologist John Edward Gray. Though the classification has changed many times, placing them at times in the Viverridae (deutsch: Schleichkatzen) family. Just 191 years later with mtDNA analysis the final clue was found and got them their own family, the Nandiniidae.
